## Interpretation

The active-boundary repair worked. Every persisted hypothesis and every APS
output stayed on `solver_design` with target
`policies/main_search_strategy.py`. There was no fallback to `baseline_policy`
or any other component surface after either a heavy Verification failure or a
zero-movement screening failure.

The run exposed a different control-plane issue. In the two later code
sessions, APS had already consumed about `44.3k/48k` observation characters
before terminal Contract preview. The generated patch's real Contract preview
was reproducibly valid and compact, but it did not fit in the remaining
session observation budget. The budget guard replaced it with a
`result_too_large` observation, and the new fail-closed self-check correctly
stopped the patch.

That means the self-check policy is right, but Contract-preview retention under
budget pressure was wrong. Terminal preview pass/fail is deterministic gate
evidence and should survive as a compact summary even when bulky preview detail
is omitted.

## Repair Actions

Implemented after this run:

1. Raise the default APS observation budget from `48,000` to `64,000`
   characters. The budget remains bounded; this only gives the normal
   problem/surface/feedback/self-check sequence more headroom.
2. Preserve a compact `proposal.contract_preview` pass/fail summary when the
   full preview payload would exceed remaining observation budget.
3. Keep fail-closed semantics for missing, skipped, or genuinely failed
   Contract previews.

Next validation should rerun the same short free-surface diagnostic. Expected
control outcome: all top-level hypotheses remain on `solver_design`, and valid
Contract previews are retained as compact pass/fail self-check evidence instead
of becoming `result_too_large` failures.
