## Interpretation

The execution-semantics repair worked mechanically: bounded destroy/repair no
longer failed mainly by budget exhaustion, recovery-only moves could be
accepted, and runtime attribution was clean. It still did not solve the real
problem: the owned components could recover from perturbations but could not
beat the repo-local phase best.

Local probes after this run showed that simply increasing rounds/top-k and
enabling all existing components still produced zero phase-best movement on
representative A-n32/A-n54 cases. Direct short reseeding of the repo-local
baseline was also not enough. The next useful repair is therefore a stronger
package-owned whole-solution primitive, not another singleton mechanism
policy.

## Follow-Up

Implemented `route_pool_recombination` under the `solver_design` main-search
boundary. It builds a route pool from complete CVRP solutions and solves a
bounded set-partitioning-style recombination problem to directly refresh phase
best. Local formal probes showed nonzero phase-best movement on P-n101-k4
after the repair.
