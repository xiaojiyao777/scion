## Interpretation

This run validates the intended control and diagnostic repair:

- forced surface stayed on `main_search_strategy`;
- structured novelty signatures prevented the prior C10 duplicate blockage;
- deep components were selected and attempted in all screened candidates;
- selected-surface runtime audit preserved the new component coverage fields;
- route-pair swap produced accepted moves and nontrivial pair-level movement.

It does not justify long validation:

- no candidate passed screening;
- case-level win rate topped out at `0.25`;
- `median_delta` stayed `0.0` in every screened round;
- `bounded_destroy_repair` accepted zero moves despite many attempts;
- one candidate failed heavy verification due to missing required runtime
  evidence.

## Next Step

The next optimization target should be `bounded_destroy_repair` efficacy, not
another surface-control repair. It is now selected and attempted, but it is
blocked by repair-budget / repair-quality behavior and produces no accepted
moves.

Recommended next slice:

- strengthen bounded destroy/repair repair budget and candidate generation
  inside the CVRP problem package;
- keep `main_search_component_coverage_status` and
  `main_search_deep_components_selected` as required audit fields;
- add or adjust controlled fixtures so destroy/repair acceptance is tested
  under the same bounded budget shape used by formal smoke candidates;
- rerun a short forced diagnostic after destroy/repair can produce accepted
  moves, and consider long validation only after screening quality improves
  beyond the current `0.0` to `0.25` case-level win-rate range.
