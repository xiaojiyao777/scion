The framework-level goal is met: both synthetic and production protocols executed end-to-end with adapter-backed objective semantics, promotion gates, synchronous weight optimization, lineage persistence, status summaries, and continued search after weight optimization.

The search-effectiveness result is mixed:

- Synthetic data: strong positive signal. 6/6 campaigns promoted at least once; 10 total structural promotions; 8/10 sync weight optimizations improved the promoted champion.

This proves v0.3 is a functioning engineering framework, but it does not prove production generalization across models. Production remains harder and more sensitive to model/code-generation quality.

## Framework Closure

### Protocol Selection

All campaigns used the intended protocol:

- synthetic -> `protocol.yaml`, version `3.0`
- production -> `protocol_prod.yaml`, version `3.1-prod`

No synthetic/production data-source mismatch was observed.

### Promotion Flow

The full gate sequence was exercised:

```text
screening -> validation -> frozen -> promote
```

Promoted production examples:

- `sonnet production s11`: `cross_subcat_merge.py`
  - screening `wr=0.667`, `md=1500`
  - validation `wr=1.0`, `md=22400`
  - frozen `wr=1.0`, `md=26600`
- `sonnet production s29`: `region_consolidate_cost.py`
  - screening `wr=0.5625`, `md=1350`
  - validation `wr=1.0`, `md=38200`
  - frozen `wr=1.0`, `md=51000`

Promoted synthetic examples:

- `sonnet synthetic s29` promoted four times, reaching `v5`.
- `gpt-mini synthetic s47` promoted twice, reaching `v3`.

### Sync Weight Optimization

Sync weight optimization behaved as intended:

- It ran after every promotion.
- It blocked the campaign until completion.
- It recorded `weight_optimizations`.
- It updated champion weight revisions when improved.
- It returned to structure search after completion.

Observed cost:

- Synthetic weight opt typically took about 35-83 minutes depending on instance/campaign load.
- Production weight opt took about 37-43 minutes when triggered.
- This confirms sync mode is semantically clean but expensive; parallelism 2 is appropriate on the current 2-core server.

### Status And Lineage

Every campaign wrote:

- `campaign_summary.json`
- `validation_runner_summary.json`
- `status.json`
- `scion.db`
- metrics artifacts
- LLM traces

No traceback or nonzero campaign exit was observed.

## Objective Guidance And Prompt Behavior

Prompt plumbing worked:

- `Objective Policy` appeared in all hypothesis traces.
- `Objective Opportunity Profile` appeared after the first screening history existed.
- `Exploration / Exploitation Control` appeared after history existed.
- Production traces consistently included `Objective Steering`, usually advising that `subcategory_splits` was stable/protected and `total_cost` should be targeted.
- Synthetic traces generally did not trigger `Objective Steering`, which is expected because `subcategory_splits` continued to move and remained a meaningful primary objective.

Parameter-search feedback appeared in synthetic campaigns after improved weight optimization. It did not appear in production because production weight optimization did not improve.

One remaining behavioral nuance: even when production prompt steering recommended total-cost targeting under protected splits, candidates sometimes still proposed direct split-consolidation mechanisms. This is acceptable exploration noise, not a framework failure, but it suggests future prompt/schema enforcement could distinguish `target_objectives` and mechanism text more strongly.

## Search Effectiveness

### Synthetic

Synthetic validation is clearly positive.

- 6/6 campaigns promoted.
- 10 total promotions.
- 8/10 weight optimizations improved the promoted champion.
- Both models can optimize the warehouse VNS algorithm on synthetic frozen-gate protocol.

This satisfies the core v0.3 demonstration that Scion can autonomously improve a combinatorial-optimization heuristic under a controlled protocol.

### Production

Production validation is positive for Sonnet but negative for GPT-mini.

Sonnet:

- 2/3 campaigns promoted.
- The promoted operators targeted cost under protected split semantics.
- Frozen improvements were large (`md=26600`, `md=51000`).
- Weight optimization completed but did not add extra improvement.

GPT-mini:

- 0/3 campaigns promoted.
- No candidate reached validation.
- Screening attempts often produced moderate median deltas but insufficient win rates.
- Verification failures were frequent, especially `V5_solution_consistency`.

Interpretation:

- The framework can optimize production instances, but production is harder and the result depends strongly on model capability.
- GPT-mini's production failure looks like candidate quality/code reliability, not protocol or framework breakage.
- Production weight optimization may have limited marginal value after strong structure changes; it completed correctly but did not improve the two Sonnet production champions.

## Failure Modes

### Verification Failures

Production GPT-mini was the main source of verification failures:

- `gpt production s11`: `V5_solution_consistency` x9
- `gpt production s29`: `V5_solution_consistency` x6, `V7_objective` x1, `V9_perf_guard` x1
- `gpt production s47`: `V5_solution_consistency` x4

Synthetic GPT-mini also showed verification fragility:

- `gpt synthetic s11`: `V5_solution_consistency` x6, `V6_feasibility` x1
- `gpt synthetic s47`: `V5_solution_consistency` x5, `V9_perf_guard` x1

Sonnet had few verification failures and produced the only production promotions.

### Family Coverage

The dominant mechanism family remains `subcategory_consolidation`, with `destroy_rebuild` as the secondary family. This means exploration is still somewhat narrow. The plateau/oscillation signals are visible and do affect proposals, but broader family diversification remains a v1 concern.

### Early Stop

Most campaigns stopped by idle-ratio early stop at round 25 or by no promotion in 25 rounds with stagnation plateau. Importantly, there was no evidence that early stop killed an already queued validation/frozen candidate in this run. Candidates that reached validation/frozen either promoted or were abandoned by the proper gate.

## Preliminary Conclusion

v0.3 is a real engineering milestone: it is no longer only a warehouse-specific prototype. The framework now executes a generic, auditable optimization loop with problem adapters, objective-policy guidance, production/synthetic protocol separation, promotion gates, and synchronous parameter optimization.

What v0.3 proves:

- Scion can autonomously improve a combinatorial-optimization heuristic on synthetic frozen-gate validation.
- Scion can improve production warehouse instances with a strong model.
- The framework lifecycle is auditable and closed end to end.

What v0.3 does not prove:

- Production success across all model classes.
- Independent causal contribution of weight optimization on production.
- Generalization to a second problem class.
- Superiority against CPLEX on all instances without a separate final champion evaluation.
